The Problem

Knowledge doesn't stay in one place.

It starts in a document, gets explained again in a meeting, and gets clarified once more in a Slack thread. Eventually it just lives in the head of whoever answered the question last.

That works, until it doesn't. The person who knew the answer leaves. The thread gets buried. A newer document supersedes an older one that nobody links back. What was once known quietly becomes unknown again.

Most businesses don't have a knowledge problem because they fail to document things. They have one because documentation, conversation, and institutional memory were never built to work as a system. Each decision was reasonable on its own, but none of them add up to something searchable.

Knowledge is one of the most valuable things a company owns, yet it's usually the least protected. It isn't backed up. It isn't structured. It isn't owned by anyone in particular. We think that's backwards. A company's knowledge should compound the way its revenue does. That means treating it as infrastructure: foundational to the business, not a byproduct of the tools it uses.

The Solution

Relativity connects your company's knowledge into one trusted system.

Relativity turns the documents, policies, training material, and everyday conversations your team already has into a single system of record your whole business can query with confidence.

Every answer comes from something your company actually approved, never a guess. Every answer says exactly where it came from, and when the answer doesn't exist yet, Relativity says so instead of inventing one.

Approved sources only.

This is what we mean by knowledge infrastructure: not another place to store documents, and not a chatbot bolted onto them, but a foundational layer that makes everything your company knows usable, starting today and expanding as your business does.
